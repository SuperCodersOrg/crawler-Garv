# Daily Design Journal · Day 14

## Section 1 – Specific Bug

Today's focus was expanding client-side hyperlink redirects, establishing network exception safety, and optimizing load performance on slow-loading websites.

I encountered a critical Boost WebSockets abort exception (`system:995`) when target website firewalls terminated connection ports, launching uncaught crashes that terminated the crawler program. Furthermore, the crawler was unable to traverse dynamic redirection landing pages using `<meta http-equiv="refresh" content="...">` instead of standard HTTP headers, and stalled indefinitely waiting for slower static tracking scripts.

---

## Section 2 – Failed Attempt

Initially, I tried to resolve the WebSocket abort crash by checking socket statuses before read calls. However, WebSocket exceptions occur asynchronously and instantly during active transmissions, bypassing state checks. I resolved this by wrapping websocket exchanges in catch-all `try/catch` scopes and resetting the connection.

When parsing the meta-refresh element, I first used standard string searches (`find`). However, case variants (e.g. `HTTP-EQUIV` vs `http-equiv`) went undetected. I resolved this by integrating KMP case-insensitive matching inside the HTMLParser. Lastly, to prevent hangs on heavy static tracking assets, I updated load handling to capture the earliest of DOMContentLoaded or complete load events.

## Section 4 – Learning Reflection

Today highlighted the importance of handling real-world network turbulence. Production websites aggressively drops connections, and relying on pure-path HTTP headers or un-guarded websocket reads is a major point of failure.

Implementing robust catch-all safety and auto-reinitialize resets keeps downstream crawlers operational, translating a system-ending crash into a simple page-fail status. Additionally, choosing when to harvest links—at the DOMContentLoaded milestone instead of waiting for heavy tracking pixels—made crawls faster and robustly immune to network lag.

By the end of the day, all WebSocket aborts were contained safely, meta-refresh redirects were resolved and parsed case-insensitively, DOMContentLoaded optimizations were verified, and all unit tests passed.
